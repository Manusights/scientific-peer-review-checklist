# Peer Review Checklist

## 1) Novelty and significance
- Is the core question clear and relevant?
- Is the contribution meaningfully different from prior work?

## 2) Methods and experimental design
- Are sample sizes justified?
- Are controls appropriate and complete?
- Are inclusion/exclusion criteria stated?

## 3) Statistical validity
- Are statistical tests appropriate for the design?
- Are effect sizes and confidence intervals reported?
- Are multiple comparisons handled correctly?

## 4) Reproducibility and transparency
- Are methods detailed enough to reproduce?
- Are code, data, and materials availability statements present?

## 5) Citations and scholarship
- Are key prior studies cited?
- Any suspicious or non-verifiable citations?

## 6) Writing clarity
- Are claims aligned with evidence?
- Are figures/tables interpretable without guesswork?
