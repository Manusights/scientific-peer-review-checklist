# How to Write High-Signal Peer Reviews

High-signal reviews help editors decide quickly and help authors improve the manuscript in one cycle.

## What high signal means
A high-signal review is:
- specific
- evidence-based
- prioritized
- actionable

## Review architecture

### Part 1: One-paragraph summary
State the claim and evidence approach in plain language.

### Part 2: Major concerns, ranked
Keep 3 to 5 major concerns max. For each concern:
- what is wrong
- why it matters
- what evidence resolves it

### Part 3: Minor concerns
Use this for clarity and presentation fixes that do not change validity.

### Part 4: Recommendation threshold
State what must change for acceptance.

## Quality checks before submit
- Can each major concern be verified?
- Is the tone neutral and professional?
- Could authors act on this feedback in one revision cycle?

## Common reviewer mistakes
- too many unranked points
- vague language without evidence requests
- mixing major and minor issues
- strong tone with weak substantiation
